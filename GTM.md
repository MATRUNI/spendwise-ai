# Go-To-Market (GTM) Strategy

## The Exact Target User
Our target user is not a generic "startup founder." The exact persona is a **Fractional CFO or VP of Finance at a Series A/B B2B SaaS company** (30-150 employees). At this stage, the company has recently secured funding, rapidly scaled headcount, and subsequently lost track of granular software provisioning. Engineers have started expensing individual Cursor and ChatGPT Plus licenses on corporate cards because IT procurement is too slow. The VP of Finance is staring at a bloated $40,000/mo credit card bill categorized simply as "Software" and is desperate for a way to instantly identify waste without bothering the engineering managers.

## The Trigger (What they Google)
They aren't searching for "AI audits." Right before they want this tool, they are actively trying to reconcile their monthly burn. They are Googling:
- *"How to manage employee ChatGPT Plus subscriptions"*
- *"GitHub Copilot Business vs Individual pricing"*
- *"SaaS spend management template for Series A"*

## Where They Hang Out
- **Slack Communities:** *Off The Ledger* (by Airbase) and *CFO Connect*.
- **Subreddits:** r/fpanda (Financial Planning & Analysis) and r/SaaS.
- **X (Twitter):** Following operators like @GergelyOrosz (Pragmatic Engineer) or @CJ_Gustafson (Run the Numbers).

## The First 100 Users (in 30 days, $0 budget)
We will not use generic content marketing. Instead, we will use a "Highly Targeted Public Tear-Down" strategy:
1. **The "Tear-Down" Thread:** We will invent a realistic dummy company ("TechCorp, 60 devs") and use our own tool to generate a public report showing how they are wasting $45,000/year on mismanaged AI seats.
2. **Direct Outreach (The Hook):** I will pull a list from Apollo.io of 300 recently funded Series A startups. I will cold-email the VP of Finance with a hyper-specific subject line: *“Are you double-paying for Copilot and Cursor?”* The email will be 3 sentences long and include a link to the public tear-down, offering to run their stack through the engine for free.
3. **Community Drops:** We will drop the tear-down into *Off The Ledger* with the framing: *"I built an instant calculator to see if your devs are double-dipping AI tools."* Finance professionals love calculators; the utility drives the click.

## The Unfair Distribution Channel
**The "Report Share" loop.** Our tool outputs a beautiful, UUID-gated `/results` page with Open Graph preview cards. The unfair advantage is that a VP of Finance cannot unilaterally cancel developer tools; they have to justify it to the VP of Engineering. We built the tool specifically so the Finance leader can drop the `/results` link into their executive Slack channel to say, *"Look at this math, we need to downgrade."* Every time a report is shared to a new executive, the Credex brand gets placed directly in front of the exact decision-makers who buy enterprise software management platforms.

## Week-1 Traction
If this works, Week-1 looks like:
- 40 completed audits (driven mostly by the cold-email campaign).
- 120 unique report views (a 3.0x viral coefficient as the 40 founders share the link with their co-founders/engineering leads).
- 4 high-savings (> $10k) Credex Consultation bookings.
